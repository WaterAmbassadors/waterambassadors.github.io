Our Initiative

Riding the Wave of Change

Water Ambassadors is a student-led conservation initiative born from a deep concern for one of Earth's most irreplaceable resources. Across our region's schools, students are stepping up as guardians of water — learning, educating peers, and driving real change in their communities.

The program focuses on three pillars: Awareness — understanding the scale of water wastage; Action — organising drives, audits, and campaigns; and Advocacy — pushing for systemic change at school and community level.

Together with the Living Peace Projects Foundation, we believe that every student can be an agent of conservation. The ripples we create today will become the waves of change tomorrow.

Cleanliness & Water Conservation Drive

Overview

The Water Ambassadors Cleanliness Drive was conducted to promote awareness about water conservation and campus cleanliness among students. The drive brought together volunteers from across school departments.

Objectives

  • Raise awareness about water wastage in everyday school activities.
  • Physically clean and audit water usage points across the campus.
  • Educate peers through demonstrations and pledge ceremonies.
  • Document and report water leakages and inefficiencies to the administration.

Activities Conducted

  • Campus walkthrough and water tap audit — identifying leaking or running taps.
  • Collection and responsible disposal of litter near water bodies on campus.
  • Student pledge-taking ceremony for responsible water use.
  • Interactive awareness session conducted by visiting coordinators from LPPF.
  • Poster and artwork exhibition on water conservation themes.

Participation

Over 100 students from multiple classes participated across two days of activities.

Key Findings

  • Several taps across washrooms were identified as leaking, reported for repair.
  • Significant littering was found near outdoor drains — cleared during the drive.
  • Students demonstrated high enthusiasm and a willingness to take responsibility.

Outcome & Next Steps

The drive was a success in building a culture of water responsibility. The initiative will be expanded to include neighbouring schools in the next phase.
