The Initiative

What is Water Ambassadors?

Water Ambassadors is a student-driven environmental initiative focused on raising awareness, fostering responsibility, and inspiring action around water conservation. Launched under the guidance of the Living Peace Projects Foundation, the programme spans multiple regional schools and creates a network of young environmental stewards.

Students who join the programme undergo workshops, conduct campus audits, create awareness campaigns, and engage with their communities to promote responsible water use. The goal is not just awareness — it is a lasting cultural shift in how young people relate to and respect water.

The programme is built on three pillars: Awareness — educating students about the global water crisis; Action — hands-on drives, audits, and campaigns; and Advocacy — amplifying the message beyond school walls.

United by a Common Purpose

Our School

A forward-thinking institution committed to holistic education, our school provided the foundation and infrastructure for the Water Ambassadors programme. With a strong culture of social responsibility, students here are encouraged to look beyond textbooks and contribute meaningfully to society.

The school's Environmental Science and Student Council departments serve as the primary organisers of on-campus activities under the Water Ambassadors umbrella.

Living Peace Projects Foundation

The Living Peace Projects Foundation (LPPF) is an international non-profit organisation dedicated to fostering sustainable development, environmental education, and community peace-building. Through programmes spanning multiple countries, LPPF empowers youth to become leaders in conservation.

Chairman Ms. Brigitte Madeleine van Baren personally championed the Water Ambassadors programme and facilitated its introduction to our school, bringing both resources and expertise to help students make a real difference.
